A note from a Liquid Lifestyles parent

Signing up is harder
than it should be.

My daughter Lyla has been swimming with you for over two years. She just moved up to Level 3.

I love what you do in the water. The way she's brought into the program isn't doing the work justice — and it's costing you families who never make it to a first lesson.

What a new parent goes through today

The current path to a first lesson

  1. 1

    Land on the site, try to figure out which class fits

    Six programs, age overlaps, level prerequisites. Most parents can't tell the difference between Liquid Bubbles, Liquid Medals Bronze, and a private lesson — and there's no one on the site asking the right questions to point them in the right direction.

  2. 2

    Try to see what times are available

    There's no schedule on the site. No way to see if the Tuesday 5pm slot at Westlake fits the family calendar. The only way to find out is to call.

  3. 3

    Pick up the phone

    Front desk answers questions, recommends a class, quotes a time. The parent says "great, I'll go register on the website." The call ends.

  4. 4

    The gap that loses the family

    Hours pass. The kid has a tough day. The spouse mentions soccer is the same night. The intent that was there at 11am is gone by 8pm. They never come back to register — and you never knew they were close.

The two roadblocks

Two questions every parent has — and the site can't answer either.

Question 1

Which class is right for my swimmer?

Today, the answer is "read all six program pages and guess." Or call. There's no guided path that asks two questions about the swimmer and points to the right class.

Question 2

When can we actually come in?

There's no published schedule by location. A parent can't see "Tuesday 5pm at Westlake has two spots open" without calling. Decision-making stops at the schedule wall.

The cost

When the site can't answer those two questions, the parent has to call — and every call introduces a delay where the sign-up can quietly die.

What the redesign fixes

The site does the work the front desk is doing today.

Step 1

A 2-minute quiz picks the class

Three questions about age, experience, and goal — then a clear recommendation. No guesswork, no comparison reading, no phone call.

Step 2

See real openings by location

Pick a location, see the schedule, see what's open this week and next. The "when can we come in" question gets answered on the page.

Step 3

Book the spot before the moment passes

Reserve and pay in the same session. The family that was ready at 11am is enrolled at 11am — not maybe-enrolled at 8pm.
