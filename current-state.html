A note from a Liquid Lifestyles parent

Your site converts
the parents who
already know.

My daughter Lyla has been swimming with you for over two years. The booking flow works fine for me. I know which class she's in and I know which pool we drive to.

The site is built for me. It isn't built for the parent who's never heard of Liquid Lifestyles — and that's where the growth is.

What's working — and what isn't

The booking flow is fine. The marketing is where the leak is.

Parents who already know what class and which pool can register in about four clicks. iClassPro handles the booking, the payment, and the schedule. That part of the funnel works. The leak is upstream — at the front of the funnel, where new families either find Liquid Lifestyles or don't, and either understand the program or don't.

  1. 1

    A parent in Bay Village Googles "swim lessons near me"

    The current site barely competes for this search. There are no location-specific landing pages, the meta data is thin, and the design signals "small local business" — not "this is the swim school for our area." Half of new families don't even see Liquid Lifestyles in the results.

  2. 2

    If they do land on the site, the first impression is dated

    A parent comparing Liquid Lifestyles to Goldfish or a YMCA next has a split-second trust judgment. The current site doesn't carry the visual weight the program itself deserves. The pool is warm and the instructors are great — the website doesn't say that loudly enough.

  3. 3

    They can't tell which class is right for their kid

    Six programs, age overlaps, level prerequisites. Most first-time parents can't tell the difference between Liquid Bubbles, Liquid Medals Bronze, and a private lesson. There's no one on the site asking the right questions and pointing them at an answer. So they back out, or they call — and a percentage of those calls never come back.

  4. 4

    The current customer is your best lead source — and the site doesn't ask

    Swim school growth is largely word-of-mouth. Parents at the daycare, the school pickup line, the neighborhood pool. The site does nothing to make a happy parent into a referral. No share link, no incentive, no "tell a friend, both get a free week." The most efficient growth channel is sitting idle.

Where the money actually is

Three growth levers the current site doesn't pull.

Lever 1

Be the answer to "swim lessons near me"

Location-specific pages, structured data, and content that ranks for the searches new parents actually run. The most underused channel — and the cheapest lead per dollar over 12 months.

Lever 2

Help first-time parents pick a class

A two-minute quiz that asks about age, experience, and goals — then names the class. Removes the biggest friction in the funnel for parents who've never set foot in your pool.

Lever 3

Turn happy parents into a lead engine

A referral program with trackable links and rewards on both sides. Lyla's mom telling another mom is your highest-converting channel — and right now nothing captures it.

The reframe

The current site captures intent that's already there. The redesign creates intent that isn't — and that's the bigger number.

What the redesign delivers

Built for the parent who hasn't decided yet.

01 · Decision support

A 2-minute quiz that picks the class for them

Three questions about age, experience, and goal — then a clear recommendation, a link to the class, and a deep-link into iClassPro with the level already filtered. The biggest single conversion lift for first-time visitors.

02 · Acquisition

Win the "swim lessons near me" search

Modern, fast, mobile-first site with location-specific landing pages for Westlake and North Royalton. Structured data, schema, and content that actually ranks. Trust signals where a cold visitor decides to stay or bounce.

03 · Frictionless handoff

Auto-select the closest location

Browser geolocation (with zip fallback) picks Westlake or North Royalton before the parent ever hits iClassPro. The deep-link carries both level and location through — half the rows, none of the confusion.

04 · Compounding growth

A referral program that runs itself

Each family gets a trackable referral link. Refer a friend — both families get a credit on their next month. The site captures the channel that's already driving most of your enrollments. Phase two work, but worth pricing now.
